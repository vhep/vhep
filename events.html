VheP Events & Opportunities

The Value in Health Economics and Policy group is constantly looking for ways to promote the work of our members. One of the key ways we do this is by hosting and organizing events with themes related to health economics and health policy. Through ongoing collaborations with our international partners we continue to produce seminars, presentations and webinar series that have a broad appeal for our members and external guests. More details on upcoming and past events can be found below:


The Geneva Papers on Risk and Insurance
Call for papers


The Geneva Association is pleased to announce a special April 2022 issue of The Geneva Papers on Risk and Insurance â€“ Issues and Practice on:

Emerging Health Risks and Insurance

We encourage you to submit contributions related to the following areas:

  • The role of insurance in managing pandemic risks
  • Insurance and the promise of digital health
  • Financing and insuring environmental health risks
  • Insurance, behavioural changes and the rise of non-communicable diseases
  • Personalised medicine and insurance coverage
  • Health insurance and population health management
  • The importance of data in predicting and managing health risks
  • Financial protection and mental health
  • The role of risk securitisation in managing health risks
  • Trends in emerging health risks and the implications for health insurance systems

    • Suggestions for other topics will be considered by the editors.

    Review process

    All contributions will go through a peer review process.
    The editors for this special issue are Christophe Courbage (Geneva School of Business Administration) and Luke Connelly (The University of Queensland; The University of Bologna).

    Further information

    Papers should be submitted electronically via the website of The Geneva Papers https://www.editorialmanager.com/gpri/ by 19 April 2021 at the latest.

    For further information on The Geneva Papers, visit http://www.palgrave-journals.com/gpp/

    For further information on this special issue, please contact Hannah Dean at hannah_dean@genevaassociation.org
